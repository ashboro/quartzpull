
## True or False Quiz: Carbon in Biological Molecules

### 1. Carbon can form only two covalent bonds.
- ❌ False  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Carbon has four valence electrons and can form four covalent bonds, enabling complex structures.
</details>

---

### 2. Condensation reactions release water when forming bonds.
- ✅ True  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Condensation reactions join monomers by releasing water and forming covalent bonds like glycosidic or peptide bonds.
</details>

---

### 3. Lactose is made of glucose and fructose.
- ❌ False  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Lactose is composed of glucose and galactose. Glucose and fructose form sucrose.
</details>

---

### 4. Phospholipids have hydrophobic heads and hydrophilic tails.
- ❌ False  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Phospholipids have hydrophilic heads and hydrophobic tails, allowing them to form bilayers in membranes.
</details>

---

### 5. Proteins are made of amino acids linked by peptide bonds.
- ✅ True  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Proteins are polymers of amino acids joined by peptide bonds formed during condensation reactions.
</details>

---

### 6. DNA contains uracil instead of thymine.
- ❌ False  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
DNA contains thymine. Uracil is found in RNA.
</details>

---

### 7. The Biuret test detects proteins by turning purple.
- ✅ True  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
The Biuret test produces a purple color in the presence of proteins due to peptide bonds.
</details>

---

### 8. Hydrolysis reactions require water to break bonds.
- ✅ True  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Hydrolysis uses water to break covalent bonds in polymers, reversing condensation.
</details>
