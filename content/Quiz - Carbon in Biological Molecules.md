
## Quiz: Carbon in Biological Molecules

### 1. Which element is central to organic molecules due to its ability to form four covalent bonds?
- [x] Hydrogen  
- [ x ] Carbon  
- [ ] Nitrogen  
- [ ] Oxygen  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Carbon has four valence electrons, allowing it to form four covalent bonds and complex structures.
</details>

---

### 2. What type of reaction forms glycosidic, peptide, and ester bonds?
- [ ] Hydrolysis  
- [x] Condensation  
- [ ] Oxidation  
- [ ] Neutralization  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Condensation reactions join monomers by releasing water and forming covalent bonds.
</details>

---

### 3. Which disaccharide is composed of glucose and fructose?
- [ ] Maltose  
- [ ] Cellulose  
- [ ] Lactose  
- [ ] Sucrose  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Sucrose is formed from one glucose and one fructose molecule.
</details>

---

### 4. What structural feature allows phospholipids to form cell membranes?
- [ ] High solubility  
- [ ] Hydrophobic tails and hydrophilic heads  
- [ ] Branched chains  
- [ ] Triple bonds  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Phospholipids have hydrophilic heads and hydrophobic tails, enabling bilayer formation in water.
</details>

---

### 5. Which level of protein structure involves hydrogen bonding to form α-helices and β-sheets?
- [ ] Quaternary  
- [ ] Secondary  
- [ ] Tertiary  
- [ ] Primary  
<details><summary>Explanation</summary>
Secondary structure refers to local folding patterns stabilized by hydrogen bonds.
</details>
